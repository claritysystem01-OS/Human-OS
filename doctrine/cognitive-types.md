# Sense-First 
## Definition 
Sense-First individuals seek signal before interpretation.

Their attention naturally moves toward what is being noticed, sensed, perceived, or detected before it has been categorized, explained, organized, or acted upon.

They often recognize that something is present before they can fully articulate what it is.

Clarity emerges through sensing reality directly and allowing patterns, principles, structures, details, and decisions to develop from what is noticed.
  
## Positive Signals 
Observable evidence that increases confidence that a classification is present. Positive signals should describe recurring patterns of attention, questioning, reasoning, or clarity formation rather than isolated behaviors or conclusions.

- Frequently notices signals, patterns, or inconsistencies before being able to fully explain them.
- Pays attention to what is being observed or sensed before attempting to categorize or interpret it.
- Often recognizes that something is significant before knowing exactly why.
- Naturally gathers impressions, observations, and signals before forming conclusions.
- Allows understanding to emerge from observation rather than immediately seeking principles, structures, details, or actions.
- Often identifies changes, anomalies, tensions, or opportunities before they are fully articulated by others.
  
## Negative Signals 
Observable evidence that reduces confidence in the classification, even if it does not increase confidence in another classification.

- Frequently moves to interpretation, explanation, organization, analysis, or action before gathering observations.
- Shows little interest in exploring what is being noticed before assigning meaning.
- Rarely identifies signals, patterns, inconsistencies, or observations before reaching conclusions.
- Appears to seek principles, structures, details, or decisions without first attending to what is present.
- Tends to form conclusions immediately rather than allowing understanding to emerge from observation.
- Does not appear to naturally attend to subtle changes, tensions, anomalies, or signals within a situation.
- Consistently prioritizes interpretation over perception. 
  
## Common Confusions
### [[Principle Oriented]]

Both categories may ultimately arrive at governing principles and may appear highly perceptive or insightful.

Sense-First seeks signal before interpretation.

Principle-Oriented seeks the governing principle that explains the information.
  
## Tie-Break Rules
### [[Principle Oriented]]

If the individual's primary search is for the governing principle, truth, or underlying rule that explains the information, classify as Principle-Oriented.

If the individual's primary search is to notice, sense, perceive, or detect what is present before assigning interpretation, classify as Sense-First.

### Medium Confidence

# Principle-Oriented 
## Definition
Principle-Oriented individuals seek the governing principle of a situation first.

They naturally look for the underlying truth, rule, pattern, or first principle that explains what is occurring.

Clarity emerges when they believe they understand what is fundamentally true.
## Positive Signals 
Observable evidence that increases confidence that a classification is present. Positive signals should describe recurring patterns of attention, questioning, reasoning, or clarity formation rather than isolated behaviors or conclusions.
- Frequently asks what is true before asking what to do.
- Searches for the underlying principle behind a situation.
- Seeks understanding of the governing logic before advancing to action.
- Evaluates options against a principle rather than convenience.
- Seeks explanations that unify multiple observations under a common principle.
## Negative Signals 
Observable evidence that reduces confidence in the classification, even if it does not increase confidence in another classification.
- Shows little interest in understanding underlying principles.
- Consistently moves to action before seeking understanding.
- Becomes impatient with discussions about governing assumptions.
- Prefers practical execution over conceptual understanding.
- Rarely asks why something works.
- Evaluates choices primarily through convenience, speed, or preference rather than principle.

# Structure-Seeker

## Definition
Structure-Seeker individuals seek an organizing framework first.

They naturally attempt to arrange information into a coherent structure, framework, sequence, or system that can be understood and navigated.

Clarity emerges when information has an understandable form.
## Positive Signals

Observable evidence that increases confidence that a classification is present. Positive signals should describe recurring patterns of attention, questioning, reasoning, or clarity formation rather than isolated behaviors or conclusions.

- Naturally organizes information into categories, frameworks, sequences, or systems.

- Attempts to understand how pieces of information relate to one another before evaluating them individually.

- Seeks an overall structure that makes information understandable and navigable.

- Frequently asks how information fits together rather than immediately asking whether it is true, meaningful, or actionable.

- Becomes more comfortable as information becomes organized into a coherent framework.

- Uses structure to reduce confusion and create clarity.

- Tends to create outlines, models, maps, frameworks, processes, or systems as a way of understanding.
## Negative Signals
Observable evidence that reduces confidence in the classification, even if it does not increase confidence in another classification.

- Shows little interest in organizing information into frameworks, systems, or structures.
- Appears comfortable operating without an overall framework or organizing model.
- Rarely attempts to understand how pieces of information relate to one another.
- Consistently focuses on individual facts, principles, meanings, or actions without seeking an overarching structure.
- Becomes impatient with efforts to categorize, map, or organize information.
- Prefers immediate understanding of a specific point rather than understanding how it fits into a larger system.
- Does not appear to gain additional clarity when information is organized into a framework or sequence.

## Definition 

Decision-Oriented individuals seek commitment and movement first.

They naturally focus on how information, opportunities, or possibilities can be advanced through choice, action, and engagement.

Their attention is drawn toward decisions, execution, implementation, and points of leverage that move a situation from possibility into reality.

Clarity emerges through determining what decision advances the situation and through engagement with the consequences of that decision.
## Positive Signals 
Observable evidence that increases confidence that a classification is present. Positive signals should describe recurring patterns of attention, questioning, reasoning, or clarity formation rather than isolated behaviors or conclusions.

- Frequently asks what decision advances the situation.

- Naturally seeks commitment, movement, or execution when evaluating possibilities.

- Identifies leverage points that convert possibility into reality.

- Tends to seek the next meaningful action rather than remaining in analysis alone.

## Negative Signals
Observable evidence that reduces confidence in the classification, even if it does not increase confidence in another classification.

- Frequently remains in observation, analysis, interpretation, or evaluation without seeking commitment or movement.
- Shows little interest in determining what decision advances a situation.
- Rarely seeks pathways that convert possibilities into action or reality.
- Appears more focused on understanding than advancing.
- Tends to defer decisions in favor of additional information, structure, principles, or details.
- Does not naturally seek leverage points that create movement or progress.
- Appears comfortable leaving possibilities unresolved without determining a path forward.
## Common Confusions
### [[Structure Seeker]]

Both categories may appear focused on progress and may ask what comes next.

Decision-Oriented seeks the decision, commitment, or movement that advances the situation.

Structure-Seeker seeks an organizing framework that makes the situation understandable and navigable.

### [[Detail Oriented]]

Both categories may engage deeply with a situation and may appear highly involved in understanding it.

Decision-Oriented seeks sufficient clarity to advance the situation through action and engagement.

Detail-Oriented seeks greater specificity, accuracy, and completeness before advancing.

If seeking advancement, then Decision Oriented.

[[Detail Oriented]]

If seeking advancement, then Decision Oriented.
